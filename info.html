The Library of Babel

In 1941 the author and librarian Jorge Luis Borges published a short story titled 'The Library of Babel'. The story describes a vast library, the books of which are filled with random combinations of letters, spaces and punctuation. The inhabitants of the library believe that the books of the library encompass every possible ordering of the letters, spaces and punctuation. The implication of this is that every possible book that could ever be written is contained somewhere within the library.
